Welcome!

Welcome to our newly developed website Falling Motion, whereby your journey into motion capture is just about to begin! If you are interested or are about to embark on your very motion capture project, look no further, for we are here to provide for you our learning center geared towards your needs and questions! At first it may be daunting but fret not for all the tools and instruction you require is but a scroll length down. Good luck and most of all, have fun!

STEP 1

START

Switch on the Vicon box to turn on the cameras. Red light should illuminate from each camera, one by one. Open Vicon IQ program and connect to Tarsus (i.e. associating the Vicon IQ program to the motion capture cameras). To connect to Tarsus, click the button with the red arrows pointing towards each other. The arrows should now be pointing away from each other.

STEP 3

Configure Cameras

The next step is to configure the cameras. In the Capture window, there is a window for all 6 cameras, otherwise click Select all Cameras. Click on Show Settings to view camera settings. Set the following values:

  • The strobe intensity should be set to 100.
  • The camera threshold should be set between 14 and 2
  • The gain should always be set to 1
  • The circle quality should be set between 30 and 40

STEP 4

Calibration I: The Wand Wave

Equipment: 240mm Wand

Calibration is used to link cameras to the environment in the room. There are two stages in the calibration process: the Wand Wave and Setting the Origin. The Wand Wave is used to position the cameras relative to each other. This process is also referred to as "painting the room".

  • Click the Calibration button
  • Under "Calibrate MX Cameras" select 3-Marker Wand(240mm)
  • Ensure "Refine Calibration" is unchecked
  • Click "Start Wand Wave"
  • After "painting" the room, press "Stop"

Once pressed, have a team member paint the wall for approximately 42-66 seconds. See video below. The wand should be loose in the team member’s hand, and allow for slight rotation while raising and lowering the wand at a steady speed. When finished, click "Stop Wand Wave". The system will continue with the calibration. Calibration must receive an "excellent" or "awesome" to continue, otherwise redo the wand wave. Tip: avoid wearing white or reflective clothing as it will affect the calibration

STEP 5

Calibration II: Setting The Origin

Equipment: L-Frame (9mm)

Now that the cameras are positioned in relation to each other, we need to position them in relation to the floor (setting the origin)! To do this

  • Place L-Frame in the center of the room with the 90 degree angle placed on the cross
  • Level the L-Frame using the two screws and sprint levels
  • Set Origin: Under "Set Volume Origin and Axes", select "Ergo_9mm_LFrame"
  • Press "Start", then wait approx. 20 seconds before pressing "Stop"

STEP 6

Markers

Equipment: Suit, 45 markers

Capture subject must put on the suit. Using the Velcro strips, ensure the suit is tight against the body. Add the markers to the suit. These markers are placed either on a bone or a joint. There should be a total of 45 markers on the body (See diagrams below). Removing or replacing markers will ruin future captures.

STEP 7

Range Of Motion

Range of motion is a process that connects the markers, template skeleton and the actual subject being captured. Without this step, you will probably not be able to make an accurate kinematic model. Refer to the video for recommended sequence of motions that should be captured. Try to be smooth and quick. The process should not take long.

  • Select the "Studio Activities" button
  • Press "capture Layout"
  • Click Start to begin capture
  • When finished, click Stop
  • Open your Data Management and click on the blue circle with the cross to load for processing
  • Click on the "Define New Actor" button and search for "ViconTrainingFullBody_Blade.vst
  • Set a character name
  • Position the time slider to where all markers are clearly visible
  • Click the "Process ROM" dropdown and select "Reconstruct ROM"
  • Slide the time slider to ensure all markers are moving smoothly
  • Click the "Process ROM" dropdown and select "T-Pose Label". The markers should be labelled
  • Click the "Process ROM" dropdown and select "Auto Label ROM" to label markers in every frame
  • Finally, calibrate the character by simply clicking "Calibrate Character"

STEP 8

Subject Capture

Once the Range of Motion has processed and the skeleton matches the subject , we can begin to capture. But first, we need to load our subject to be captured.

  • Select the "Capture" tab
  • Select "Activate Objects"
  • Check the box under Available Objects to activate the subject

Now we’re ready to capture!

  • Under "Capture Layout" select "Capture & Take Info"
  • You can start the capture under the Capture Group section
