What is Motion Capture?

A relatively brand new technique developed in the mid 1970’s as the emergence of technology such as the computer became more widely available to the public. The very meaning of motion capture is the process of capturing an object’s movement, such as an actor, and translating it into a digital format which will be then used for either 2D or 3D animations. Some of the advantages of using motion capture in a project range from obtaining real time results, reducing costs of frame based animation such as hand drawn technique, allows for multiple tests and different delivery styles based on the actor's skill level, and finally more results can be produced in a shorter amount of time compared to traditional animation methods. By combining these advantages it can lead to reducing cost and meeting deadlines, as a result, this makes motion capture an appealing option as newer technology is being developed.
